About Us




“

A good product starts with a simple question. The question which would lead to innovation. And with the right creativity, that innovation would lead to a concept. After rigorous testing, it would then become a final product.

”

Furina Innovations was founded in 1992 by Mohammad Hatif Sabiri, a visionary computer scientist, and Richard Gilbert, a skilled mathematician, in Singapore, a burgeoning technology hub. The duo was inspired by the idea of making cutting-edge computing technology accessible to people worldwide. Their shared vision was to create hardware that was not only high-performing but also durable, energy-efficient, and aesthetically designed to fit seamlessly into both professional and personal spaces.

Initially, the company focused on motherboards, which were gaining traction as personal computers became household essentials. Furina's first product, the VTX-1, was a compact, affordable, and highly reliable motherboard. Its launch was a game-changer for tech enthusiasts, who praised its stability and overclocking capabilities. This success propelled the company into the spotlight, earning it partnerships with early PC manufacturers.

As the market for portable computing surged, Furina released its first laptop, the VT-Nova, in 1998. The Nova line was renowned for its lightweight design, robust build, and exceptional battery life, earning it accolades in the business and education sectors. By 2003, Furina had solidified its position as a global competitor, rivaling established brands like ASUS, Dell, and Lenovo.
